    Siena Poll: New Yorkers Support Using Federal Land to Shelter Migrants, NOT Building the Southern Border Wall, Amid Misconceptions About Immigration and Bias Questions

    NOTE Besides being a small sample size (800), Sienna uses "a proprietary online panel" (384). In addition, all the questions were badly formed and wreak of bias & ignorance (see CROSS TABS). Press release for the poll (PR in pdf) (CROSS TABS)

    dailywire.com More than four in ten New York state residents – 41% – said they support a border wall spanning the entire length of the country's southern border with Mexico, according to a Siena College Poll released Tuesday. ::: Even in New York City, 38% of city dwellers said they support a border wall.

    thepostmillennial.com Despite being hit particularly hard by the recent wave of migrants, New York City residents held similar views to those living in the suburbs and upstate.

    washingtonexaminer.com More than 75% of respondents said most immigrants want only to build a better life and that the country was built by people around the world, according to the Sept. 5-8 telephone and online survey by Siena College. ::: Seventy-two percent said immigrants bring vitality to America, and 60% believed businesses need immigrants to be successful. Slightly more people disagreed with the statement that immigrants take in more resources than they return in economic activity – 48% to 42%.

    ny1.com By a 56%-36% margin, Siena poll respondents said they were in favor of the use of federally-owned land and buildings as shelters, and 59% said they were in favor of making it easier for migrants to get work authorizations. ::: The poll was conducted from Sept. 5-8, polling 414 New Yorkers via phone and 386 more from "a proprietary online panel of New Yorkers." The results have a margin of error of +/- 3.8 percentage points. For more results, click here.
